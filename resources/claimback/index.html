ClaimBack: The Lawyer Who Quit Law to Recover India's Unclaimed Assets

Abhijith Jajio spent years practicing law before he noticed a problem hiding in plain sight across India: enormous sums of money and property sitting unclaimed, simply because the rightful heirs didn't know it existed or didn't know how to claim it.

Unclaimed ancestral assets — old bank deposits, insurance payouts, shares, provident fund balances, property — are a genuinely large, underreported problem in India. Money tied to a grandparent or distant relative sits in a bank or registry for years, sometimes decades, because the people legally entitled to it have no straightforward way to discover it exists, let alone navigate the legal process to claim it.

The lawyer's-eye view of a legal-system problem

This is exactly the kind of problem that's easy to overlook unless you've sat on the inside of the legal system that creates it. Abhijith's law background meant he understood firsthand how opaque and procedurally heavy the claims process actually is — not an abstract inefficiency, but a concrete, repeated pattern he would have seen play out in real cases.

Recognizing the problem from inside a law career is one thing. Deciding to leave that career to build a company around fixing it is a different, much larger decision — and it's the decision that separates ClaimBack's origin from a lawyer simply complaining about an inefficient system.

Why AI, and why now

The traditional path to recovering an unclaimed ancestral asset involves manually searching across disconnected institutions — banks, insurers, government registries — each with their own records and processes, with no unified way to check if a deceased relative left something unclaimed. ClaimBack's AI-powered approach is built to make that search and matching process dramatically faster and more accessible than the manual, lawyer-by-lawyer process Abhijith would have seen from the inside.

The best founders aren't always the ones with the most novel idea. Sometimes they're the ones who spent years inside a broken system and finally decided to fix it instead of just working around it.

₹6.5 Cr and counting

ClaimBack has helped recover ₹6.5 crore in assets to date — real money returned to real families who, in many cases, likely had no idea it existed. That figure represents the platform's core promise actually working at meaningful scale, not just a product that's technically functional.

Building inside a structured cohort

Like other LaunchPilot founders, Abhijith built ClaimBack alongside a cohort of founders working on completely different problems — a useful test for any founder coming from a specialized professional background like law: explaining a niche, system-specific problem clearly enough for non-specialists to immediately grasp why it matters is a skill that pays off again later, with investors, partners, and customers who also won't start out knowing the inside of India's asset registry system.
