Best MBA Programs for Working Professionals Who Want to Start a Business

LaunchPilot Resources 9 min read Updated June 2026

Not every "MBA for entrepreneurs" actually helps you build one while you still have a job. Here's an honest look at the real options — including where we fit, and where we don't.

If you're employed and thinking about starting a company, the MBA landscape splits into two real categories: traditional business schools with an entrepreneurship concentration, and founder-first programs built specifically around building a company while you study. Both can work. They're just solving different problems. Below are five real programs worth knowing about, what each one is actually best for, and how to think about choosing between them.

What to actually look for

Before the list — three things matter more than brand name if your goal is to start a company, not just get a credential:

  • Format that survives a real job. Evening/weekend or fully async beats anything requiring you to disappear for a semester.
  • Who's actually teaching you. Operating founders and investors give different value than career academics — both have a place, but know which you're getting.
  • What your classmates are doing. A cohort full of other builders is a different network than a cohort full of people going back to corporate roles.

The programs

01

Babson College — MBA, Entrepreneurial Leadership

Format: Part-time onlineBest for: Brand-name credential + deep entrepreneurship focus

Widely regarded as one of the most entrepreneurship-focused business schools in the world, with the curriculum and culture built specifically around founder leadership rather than general management. Strongest pick if a recognized brand name matters for your situation alongside the entrepreneurial focus.

02

NYU Stern — Part-Time MBA, Online/Modular Option

Format: Hybrid — online + in-person intensivesBest for: Wanting an Ivy-adjacent network without leaving your job

A genuinely flexible modular structure with real in-person cohort time, which matters if you want founder peers you actually meet face-to-face periodically rather than purely online.

03

FIU Business — Professional MBA Flex, Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Format: Evening, one night/week + residenciesBest for: US-based professionals wanting structured weekly rhythm

A specific entrepreneurship and innovation specialization with mandatory professional development residencies — useful if you want forced in-person networking checkpoints built into the calendar rather than leaving it optional.

04

LaunchPilot — Founder MBA

Format: Part-time, fully onlineBest for: Already have a thesis, want to build it now, not after graduating

Where LaunchPilot is genuinely different from the three above: the company you build is the coursework, not a side project alongside it. Taught by founders who've shipped, not professors who've read case studies about it. The honest tradeoff: less brand-name recognition than Babson or NYU, more direct compression toward actually launching something during the program itself.

05

USC Marshall — Online MBA

Format: Online + one-week on-campus residencyBest for: Wanting cohort community without going fully remote

A strong middle-ground option if full remote feels too isolating — the mandatory residency week forces the in-person community most fully-online programs lack.

The honest answer to "which is best" is: it depends on whether you want a brand-name credential, a peer network of builders, or the fastest path to actually shipping something.

How to actually choose

If brand recognition for future fundraising or hiring conversations matters most to you, Babson or NYU Stern carry more weight in a pitch deck's "About the Founder" slide. If you want forced in-person structure, FIU or USC Marshall's residency model gives you that without relocating. If you already have an idea and want the program itself to be the thing that gets it built — not a credential you finish and then start building after — that's the specific gap LaunchPilot's Founder MBA is built to fill.
