Gamma: Build an Investor-Ready Pitch Deck in an Afternoon

LaunchPilot Resources 5 min read Updated June 2026
Best forInteractive, web-based decks
Free tierYes, limited credits
Key featureViewer engagement analytics

Most founders spend 20-40 hours building a pitch deck by hand. Gamma gets you a structured first draft in minutes — leaving your time for the part that actually matters: the story.

Gamma is an AI presentation tool that turns a plain-text description of your company into a structured, designed deck — including problem, solution, market sizing, and traction slides — in a web-based, interactive format. It also includes viewer analytics, showing you which slides an investor actually spent time on, which is genuinely useful intelligence for a follow-up conversation.

What investors actually care about

A clean, professional-looking deck is table stakes, not a differentiator — investors evaluate the substance of your business, not whether a human designer placed every element by hand. What an AI-generated deck can't do for you is the actual thinking: your specific numbers, your real traction, and a story that's true to your company. Gamma handles structure and design; you still own the content.

Quick how-to: from blank page to first draft

Step 1

Write your own outline first, in plain text

Before touching Gamma, write a few sentences for each section: problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask. This forces you to actually think through your own narrative rather than letting the tool's default structure think for you.

Step 2

Generate the first draft from that outline

Paste your outline into Gamma and let it generate a full deck structure and design. Treat this as a rough first draft to react to, not a finished product — you're looking for a structure to edit, not a deck to send as-is.

Step 3

Rewrite every slide's text in your own voice

Generic AI phrasing is a red flag to experienced investors who've seen hundreds of AI-generated decks by now. Replace generated text with your own specific numbers, your own customer quotes, and your own way of describing the problem — the structure can stay; the words should be yours.

Step 4

Share the link and watch the analytics

Send the live Gamma link rather than a static PDF, and check which slides investors actually spend time on. If everyone lingers on your traction slide, that's the part of your story to lead with verbally in the next conversation.

The trap to avoid

Don't let deck polish substitute for the actual fundraising readiness work — a beautiful deck with weak, vague traction numbers convinces nobody. Spend the time AI saves you on building real traction and getting your numbers right, not on perfecting slide transitions.
