01

Who we are and what this policy covers

LaunchPilot School of Business Pte. Ltd. ("LaunchPilot", "we", "us", "our") is a private education institution registered in Singapore. We operate the website launchpilotschool.com and associated program pages, and we offer MBA and founder programs online and in person.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share personal data when you visit our website, apply for a program, enrol as a student, or interact with us in any other way. It also explains your rights under the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA) and, where applicable, the European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Plain English summary: We collect your data when you fill out forms, apply to our programs, or visit our website. We use it to manage your application and enrollment, send you program information, and improve our marketing. We do not sell your personal data to anyone. Ever.

03

How we use your personal data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Processing your program application and communicating its outcome
  • Managing your enrollment, coursework, and student records
  • Processing tuition payments and issuing receipts
  • Sending you program updates, schedule changes, and learning materials
  • Providing access to our AI Learning System and online platform
  • Matching you with a mentor based on your profile and goals
  • Sending marketing communications about our programs (with your consent, or where we have a legitimate interest)
  • Running targeted advertising campaigns on Meta, Google, and LinkedIn
  • Analysing website traffic and improving our user experience
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Fraud prevention and platform security

Our legal basis for processing under PDPA is generally consent (for marketing), contractual necessity (for enrollment and program delivery), and legitimate interests (for security and analytics). Where we rely on legitimate interests, we have assessed that they do not override your rights.

04

Advertising, Meta Pixel & remarketing

We use advertising tools to promote our programs and to reach people who may be interested in founder education. This section explains exactly what that means for your data.

Note: Our Meta (Facebook/Instagram) advertising account has been subject to restrictions. This policy reflects our data practices including the advertising tools we use and your rights regarding them.

Meta Pixel (Facebook/Instagram)

Our website uses the Meta Pixel — a small piece of code that tracks your activity on our site (pages viewed, forms started, applications submitted) and sends this data to Meta. We use this to:

  • Measure the effectiveness of our Facebook and Instagram ads
  • Show retargeting ads to people who have visited our website
  • Build "lookalike audiences" of people similar to our applicants
  • Optimise ad delivery to people most likely to apply

The data sent to Meta via the Pixel may include your IP address, browser information, and event data (e.g. "viewed pricing page", "started application"). This is processed under Meta's own privacy policy. You can opt out of Meta's use of this data for advertising via your Facebook Ad Settings.

Google Ads & Analytics

We use Google Ads to run search and display advertising, and Google Analytics 4 to understand how visitors use our website. Google may use cookies to serve personalised ads. You can opt out via Google's ad settings or by installing the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

LinkedIn Insight Tag

We use the LinkedIn Insight Tag to track conversions from LinkedIn campaigns and to enable retargeting to LinkedIn members who visit our site. You can opt out via LinkedIn's opt-out page.

Conversion APIs

In addition to browser-based pixels, we use server-side Conversion APIs (Meta CAPI, Google Enhanced Conversions) to send conversion data directly from our servers to the ad platforms. This improves measurement accuracy and reduces reliance on browser cookies. The data shared is hashed (encrypted) before transmission.

06

How long we keep your data

  • Applicants (not enrolled): 2 years from application date, then deleted or anonymised
  • Enrolled students: 7 years from program completion for academic records; financial records retained as required by Singapore law
  • Marketing contacts: Until you unsubscribe, or 3 years from last engagement (whichever is sooner)
  • Website analytics: 26 months (Google Analytics default retention)
  • Ad platform data: Subject to Meta, Google, and LinkedIn's own retention policies
  • Payment data: As required by MAS and IRAS regulations (minimum 5 years)
07

Your rights

Under the PDPA (and GDPR where applicable), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Correction: Request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Withdrawal of consent: Withdraw consent for marketing at any time (this does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal)
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data where we have no legal obligation to retain it
  • Portability: Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format (GDPR only)
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Opt out of marketing: Unsubscribe from any email via the link in every email, or email us directly

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@launchpilotschool.com. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

If you are located in the EU/EEA and believe your rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

International data transfers

LaunchPilot is headquartered in Singapore and serves students globally. Many of our third-party service providers (including Meta, Google, Anthropic, and Stripe) are based in the United States.

When we transfer data outside Singapore, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with the PDPA, including contractual clauses that require recipients to protect your data to a standard comparable to Singapore law.

For transfers from the EU/EEA, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission.
